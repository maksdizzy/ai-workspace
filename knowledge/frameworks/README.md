# Consulting Frameworks

Quick reference for common frameworks. Use the right tool for the job.

## Problem Structuring
- **Issue Tree** — Break problem into MECE components. Start with "Why?" or "How?"
- **Hypothesis-Driven** — Form a hypothesis first, then test. Faster than open exploration.
- **First Principles** — Strip away assumptions, reason from ground truth

## Strategy
- **Porter's Five Forces** — Industry attractiveness (buyers, suppliers, substitutes, new entrants, rivalry)
- **Value Chain** — Where is value created? Where are margins?
- **SWOT** — Quick strategic snapshot (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
- **Blue Ocean** — Find uncontested market space (value innovation)
- **Ansoff Matrix** — Growth strategy (market penetration, development, product dev, diversification)

## Customer/User
- **Jobs to Be Done** — What "job" is the customer hiring the product for?
- **Customer Journey Map** — End-to-end experience, pain points, moments of truth
- **Segmentation** — Group by behavior, needs, or value (not just demographics)

## Operations
- **Lean / Value Stream Mapping** — Identify waste, optimize flow
- **Theory of Constraints** — Find and fix the bottleneck
- **RACI** — Clarify roles (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)

## Decision Making
- **Decision Matrix** — Weighted scoring across criteria
- **2x2 Matrix** — Quick prioritization (impact vs effort, urgency vs importance)
- **Pre-mortem** — "Imagine this failed — why?" Surfaces risks proactively

## Communication
- **Pyramid Principle** — Answer first, then supporting arguments (Minto)
- **SCQA** — Situation, Complication, Question, Answer (story structure for memos)
- **So What? Test** — Every slide/point must pass "So what?" and "Why does this matter?"

## Financial
- **Unit Economics** — CAC, LTV, margins at the unit level
- **DCF** — Discounted cash flow for valuation
- **Sensitivity Analysis** — What changes the outcome most?
